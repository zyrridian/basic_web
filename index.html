Is Oxygen a Slow-Acting Poison?

We breathe oxygen to survive, but what if it's actually killing us slowly? Oxygen plays a vital role in cellular respiration, but it also causes oxidative stress, which leads to aging and cellular damage. Over time, this accumulation contributes to diseases and even our eventual death. Could we ever survive without it?
